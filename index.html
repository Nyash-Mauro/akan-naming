The Akan Naming System

Akan naming system,this is normally used to newborns in West Africa,Ivory Coast Ghana and Jamaica in the carribean.The Akan people of Ghana frequently name their children after the day of the week they were born and the order in which they were born. These "day names" have further meanings concerning the soul and character of the person. Middle names have considerably more variety and can refer to their birth order, twin status, or an ancestor's middle name.

The Akan people use the Kwa language group’s system of timekeeping, which is based on a six-day week (‘nnanson‘),the seventh day is inclusive.Sunday-born ('Kwasíada') are named as Kwasi (male) or Akosua (female),which is associated with the Universe.Monday-born('Ɛdwóada') are named as Kwadwó (male) or Adwoa (female),which is associated with Peace.Tuesday-born('Ɛbénada')are named as Kwabená(male) or Abena, Abina, Abénaa(female), which is associated with the Ocean.Wednesday-born('Wukúada') are named as Kwakú(male) or Akua, Akúá(female),which is associated with a spider.Thursday-born('Yáwóada') are named as Yaw(male) or Yaa(female),which is associated with the Earth.Friday-born('Efíada') are named as Kofí, Koffi(male) or Afua(female),which is associated with fertility.Saturday-born('Méméneda') are named as Kwámè, Kwǎmè(male) or Ama, Ám̀ma, Ámmá(female),which is associated with God.

This system is also used by Ndyuka people of Suriname.They are of of six Maroon("Bush Negro") groups in Suriname.Sunday-born are named Kwasi, Kwasiba. Monday-born are named Kodyo, Adyuba. Tuesday-born are named Abeni. Wednesday-born are named Kwaku, Akuba. Thursday-born are named Yaw, Yaba. Friday-born are named Kofi, Afiba. Saturday-born are named Kwami, Amba.
